Designing Experience and Interaction for Vehicles in the Age of Automation

Ph.D. Design Research Project
2020-present
Industrial Design | Technical University of Eindhoven
Promoter: Marieke Martens, Pavlo Bazilinskyy (previous: Bastian Pfleging)

Motivation

I obtained a B.Eng degree in Automotive Engineering at Tongji University and a M.Sc degree in Industrial Design at TU Eindhoven, specializing in Human-Computer Interaction (HCI). I am passionate about exploring the intersection of technology and user experience in the automotive industry. I am driven to bridge the gap between humans and autonomated vehicles through carefully designed interaction that enhance user experience. Combining my interdisciplinary knowledge, I am excited to contribute to the future of interaction design within the automotive context through this Ph.D. program.


Research Topic

Non-intrusive In-Vehicle Interaction in Non-Critical Automated Driving Scenarios Automated vehicles are capable of performing all driving tasks under certain restricted conditions, allowing the driver to temporarily disengage from driving and conduct non-driving-related activities. Due to the diversion of attention from the road and lack of anticipation, people may be negatively surprised by the vehicle's actions in normal situations, despite there being no emergencies or takeover requests. Consequently, an important research question revolves around how the vehicle can effectively interact with users, mitigating automation surprises without frequently disrupting their non-driving-related activities.


To address this issue, I adopt the research-through-design (RtD) approach, which involves the design, development, and evaluation of prototypes. By conducting an on-road qualitative study employing a Wizard-of-Oz (WoZ) vehicle, I explore the driving experience in non-critical automated scenairos and gain insight into the design of non-intrusive interaction. Following the notion of Calm Technology proposed by Mark Weiser in the 90's, I aim to propose new interaction techniques for vehicles in the age of automation.
